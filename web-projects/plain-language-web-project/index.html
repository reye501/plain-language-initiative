What is a Plain Language Summary?

A plain language summary combines a clinical trial's results with plain language writing style. Plain language is a style of writing that considers the audience's reading level. Writing in plain language includes getting to the point without unneccessarily complex scientific terms, good organization and layout, and knowing the audience you are writing for. Clinical trial results are often given in scientific journals or on clinicaltrials.gov in a way that is hard for the average person to understand. As a result, many people who are interested in the trial's results cannot fully comprehend themâ€” even people who participated in the trial. The goal of this project is to encourage the use of plain language in clinical trial results so that anyone who is interested in them can read them. Finally, because participants of clinical trials sacrifice so much for the sake of developing new treatments, they deserve to have access to trial results in an understandable manner.
